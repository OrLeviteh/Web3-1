Yoga Sun Salutation

A STEP-BY-STEP TUTORIAL

The sun salutation is a series of postures performed in a single, graceful flow. Each movement is coordinated with the breath (inhale as you stretch ,exhale as you fold or contract). The series works every part of the body while increasing flexibility. It is also a way to warm up the body and prepare for deeper yoga postures.


There are many variations of sun salutation. Below is an overview of the main postures you will find in each variation. Click on a step below to get more detailed information.

Tadasana

MOUNTAIN POSE

It may seem like you are just standing there, but bringing the body into alignment is hard work. The alignment for Tadasana carries in to many of the standing and inverted poses.

  1. 1. Come to stand with big toes touching.
  2. 2. Lift up all your toes and fan them out, creating a solid base.
  3. 3. Bring your weight evenly onto all four corners of both feet.
  4. 4. Root feet and calves down into the ground.
  5. 5. Engage the quadriceps and draw them upward.
  6. 6. Tuck your tailbone under and draw in the belly.
  7. 7. Widen collar bones and make sure shoulders are parallel to pelvis.
  8. 8. Keep your neck long, crown of the head towards the ceiling, and shoulder blades down the back.

Urdhva Hastasana

RAISED HANDS POSE

Inhale and bring the arms up over head.

  1. 1. From Tadasana, bring your arms out to the side and up.
  2. 2. Press the palms together, keep the arms straight and gaze towards your hands.
  3. 3. Slide the shoulder blades down the back.
  4. 4. Maintain your alignment.

Uttanasana

STANDING FORWARD BEND OR 'HEAD TO KNEES'

Exhale and fold forward over the legs.

  1. 1. From Urdhva Hastasana, swan dive the arms out to the side while folding forward.
  2. 2. Make sure the fold comes from the hips (and hip creases) and not from the back.
  3. 3. Bring the fingertips in line with the toes and press the palms flat.
  4. 4. Engage the quadriceps muscles of the thighs. The more you use your quads, the more the hamstrings (the muscles on the back of the thighs) will open up.
  5. 5. Bring your weight a little bit forward into the balls of your feet so that your hips stay over the ankles.
  6. 6. Let your head hang.

Ardha Uttanasana

FLAT BACK

Inhale. Continue to engage your quadriceps as in the previous poses.

  1. 1. Lengthen the spine.
  2. 2. Lift your head and chest up and look forward
  3. 3. Make sure your back stays flat and does not become rounded.

Chaturanga Dandasana

FOUR LIMBED STAFF POSE

Exhale. Flatten your palms and jump or step back to a plan position to get into Chaturanga.

  1. 1. From plank position, bend the arms straight back, keeping the upper arms hugging into your sides.
  2. 2. Lower down toward the floor, stopping when your forearms and upper arms are at a right angle.
  3. 3. Keep the whole body very level.
  4. 4. Push back into the heels.
  5. 5. Roll over or tuck the toes when you want to move into downward facing dog.

Urdhva Mukha Svasana

UPWARD FACING DOG

Inhale.Roll over the toes into upward dog.

  1. 1. From Chaturanga Dandasana, come forward rolling over the toes without letting the thighs touch the floor.
  2. 2. Keep the legs engaged and off of the floor, while pressing the tops of the feet down and dropping the hips.
  3. 3. Make sure the shoulders stay over the wrists and are not hunched up near the ears.

Adho Mukha Svansana

DOWNWARD FACING DOG

Exhale. Roll over the toes into down dog. Take 5 breaths.

  1. 1. Come to your hands and knees with the wrists underneath the shoulders and the knees underneath the hips.
  2. 2. Curl the toes under and push back raising the hips and straightening the legs.
  3. 3. Spread the fingers and ground down from the forearms into the fingertips.
  4. 4. Outwardly rotate the upper arms broadening the collarbones.
  5. 5. Let the head hang, move the shoulder blades away from the ears towards the hips.
  6. 6. Engage the quadriceps strongly to take the weight off the arms, making this a resting pose
